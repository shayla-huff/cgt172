What Can A Starship Do?

2025 — Data Collection / Analysis / Prototyping / Evaluation

In this team-based UX design project, we explored how Purdue’s Starship delivery robots could be redesigned to support services beyond food delivery and create new kinds of human-to-human connection on campus. We conducted background research and gathered real user insight through narrative and observational methods, journey mapping, and at least four audio-recorded interviews (one per team member). Using our findings, we generated and refined non-food Starship concepts (e.g., campus services, messaging, lost & found, navigation, or games), then iteratively developed the idea through sketches, storyboards, and wireframes designed to shift users’ mental models of what a Starship can do. Finally, we evaluated the design with at least three users and used feedback to improve the solution before presenting it as a pitch and compiling full process documentation with research, concepts, prototypes, and evaluation results.
